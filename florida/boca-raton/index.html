Questions to ask

Before choosing care in Boca Raton, get clear on the basics.

The goal is not to make every decision today. The goal is to ask better questions so your family can move with more confidence.

For in-home care

  • What daily tasks does your loved one need help with?
  • Are there minimum hours or schedule requirements?
  • Can care happen overnight, on weekends, or after hospital discharge?
  • How are caregivers screened, trained, and replaced if needed?
  • Can the care plan change if needs increase?

For memory or safety concerns

  • Has wandering, confusion, or medication safety become a concern?
  • Are falls or nighttime movement creating risk?
  • Would home safety updates or monitoring help?
  • Does the caregiver need respite or backup coverage?
  • Are legal planning documents already in place?

For legal or benefits questions

  • What documents should be gathered before the first call?
  • Is the question about disability, estate planning, guardianship, or care costs?
  • Are there deadlines, appeal windows, or eligibility rules?
  • How are fees handled?
  • What should the family understand before signing anything?

For planning ahead

  • What expenses are you trying to prepare for?
  • Who in the family needs to be part of the conversation?
  • What coverage, documents, or resources are already in place?
  • What decisions can wait, and what needs attention soon?
  • What should be reviewed with a qualified professional?
