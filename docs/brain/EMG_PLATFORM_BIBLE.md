# EMG_PLATFORM_BIBLE.md

**The EMG InMyCity Platform Operating System**
*Shared operating principles for every EMG local-intent property.*

---

## 0. How to Use This Document

This is the top-level "platform brain" for Elite Media Group (EMG). Every InMyCity property (CareInMyCity, PetsInMyCity, HomesInMyCity, ServicesInMyCity, FoodInMyCity, ActivitiesInMyCity, MarriagesInMyCity, and future brands) inherits these rules.

When a brand-specific document conflicts with this file, the brand document wins **only** for brand voice/content. For monetization, compliance, trust, indexing, and "never do" rules, **this file is authoritative**.

Audience: future Claude/AI agents, developers, writers, and EMG operators. Treat every rule as actionable. When in doubt, prefer user trust over revenue.

---

## 1. EMG Mission

Elite Media Group builds **trusted, AI-assisted, local-intent resource platforms** that help ordinary people make high-stakes local decisions with clarity and confidence.

We are not a content farm. We are not an ad-arbitrage operation. We build durable, useful, EEAT-strong properties that earn organic trust, rank because they deserve to, and monetize as a byproduct of genuine usefulness.

**One-line mission:** *Help real people in real cities solve real local problems, with an AI guide at their side.*

Brands look and feel distinct, but the **engine is shared**. Build once, reuse everywhere.

## 3. Local-Intent Strategy

We target queries with **local + transactional or informational intent**: "{service} in {city}", "{city} {service} options", "how to choose {service} near me".

Rules:
- Every property is organized around **State → City → Service** pages.
- Pages must serve a **specific** local intent. Generic national content alone is not enough.
- A page exists only if a real person would plausibly search for it.
- We win by **coverage + uniqueness + trust**, not by volume of thin pages.

---

## 4. User-First Resource Philosophy

The user comes before the ad, the affiliate link, and the conversion.

- Lead with **help**, not with monetization.
- Public, free, government and nonprofit resources are surfaced first.
- Never gate genuinely useful information behind a wall, signup, or upsell.
- Never create anxiety to drive a click ("fear-to-sell" is banned across all brands).

If a page would embarrass us if a family member read it, it does not ship.

---

## 5. AI-Assisted Navigation

Every property ships an AI guide (Carl for CareInMyCity; equivalent personas elsewhere).

Platform rules for all AI guides:
- The guide **assists navigation and understanding**; it does not give professional advice (medical, legal, financial, insurance).
- The guide is **conversational, not a quiz**.
- The guide escalates real emergencies to appropriate services (e.g., 911, crisis lines).
- The guide uses location context to point to relevant local pages and public resources.
- The guide cross-links to sibling InMyCity properties when genuinely relevant.

## 6. Brand Architecture

- **Elite Media Group (EMG)** is the parent company and owner of all properties.
- Each **InMyCity** name is a product brand.
- Footer/legal: the product brand is the experience; **EMG is the owning company**.
- Standard copyright form: `© {YEAR} Elite Media Group. All Rights Reserved.`
- Cross-brand links are encouraged where relevant, but each brand keeps its own identity, voice, and design tokens.

---

## 7. Trust Principles (EEAT at the Platform Level)

1. **Experience** — content reflects real-world understanding of the vertical.
2. **Expertise** — content is accurate, sourced, and reviewed.
3. **Authoritativeness** — author/organization attribution is present and consistent.
4. **Trustworthiness** — disclaimers, transparency, and honest framing on every page.

Required trust elements on every property: visible About page, founder/author attribution, clear disclaimers, real contact path, consistent Organization schema, and honest sourcing.

---

## 8. Monetization Principles

- Monetization **follows** trust and usefulness; it never leads.
- Pages must provide standalone value **before** any ad or affiliate consideration.
- No page should be built **only** to host ads.
- Ad density must never harm readability, mobile UX, or Core Web Vitals.
- Affiliate and ad placements never masquerade as editorial recommendations.

---

## 9. AdSense Principles

- AdSense only on pages with **substantive, original, useful** content.
- Never place ads on thin, placeholder, error, or under-construction pages.
- Respect AdSense policies: no ads near misleading buttons, no accidental-click layouts, no prohibited content.
- Maintain a clean content-to-ad ratio.
- Keep the AdSense loader centralized and untouched by content edits (see protected systems in brand brains).
- Indexing readiness and AdSense readiness go together: real pages, real value, real navigation.

---

## 10. Affiliate Principles

- Disclose affiliate relationships clearly and near the link.
- Only recommend categories of products/services that genuinely help the user.
- Never let affiliate incentives distort editorial guidance.
- Affiliate content must still meet the property's editorial and EEAT standards.
- No affiliate links inside AI guide responses unless explicitly designed, labeled, and compliant.

---

## 11. Compliance Principles

- **No professional advice.** Properties provide educational information and public resources only — not medical, legal, financial, or insurance advice.
- Every relevant page carries the appropriate disclaimer.
- Respect all applicable advertising, privacy, and accessibility standards.
- Honor user privacy: minimize data collection, be transparent, never sell sensitive personal data.
- Follow platform policies for Google, Bing, AdSense, and affiliate networks.

---

## 12. Content Quality Rules

A page ships only if it is:
- **Real** — represents an actual place/service combination.
- **Unique** — not a name-swap clone of another page.
- **Useful** — answers the user's likely question end-to-end.
- **Sourced** — cites public/government/nonprofit resources where relevant.
- **Readable** — clear structure, scannable headings, mobile-friendly.
- **Honest** — no exaggeration, no fear-selling, no fake scarcity.

Thin content, duplicate content, and "phantom" pages (URLs with no real page behind them) are prohibited.

---

## 13. Internal Linking Standards

- Every page links **up** (to its parent state/service), **across** (to sibling pages), and **down** (to relevant child pages) where applicable.
- No orphan pages: every public page must be reachable from navigation and/or contextual links.
- Service pages link to related services; city pages link to other services in that city and to the parent state.
- Cross-brand links are contextual and relevant, never spammy.
- Anchor text is descriptive, not "click here".

---

## 14. UX Standards

- Mobile-first. Every layout must work on small screens.
- Fast: protect Core Web Vitals; defer non-critical scripts.
- Accessible: semantic HTML, alt text, sufficient contrast, keyboard navigation.
- Consistent components: cards, buttons, headers, footers share a design language per brand.
- The AI guide widget must never block content or trap focus.

---

## 15. Footer / Header Standards

**Header:** brand mark + primary nav (Home, About, Search, core tool) + AI guide entry point.

**Footer:** professional multi-column layout. Standard column pattern:
- **{Brand}** — Home, About, Contact, Search
- **Resources** — primary tool, secondary tools, directory, Sitemap
- **{Vertical} Services** — the property's core service categories
- **Legal** — Privacy Policy, Terms, Disclaimer

**Footer never** contains a random short list of states/cities as if they were the whole product. Bottom line: `© {YEAR} Elite Media Group. All Rights Reserved.`

Footer/header are presentation-layer and may be upgraded; engineering systems they sit near (analytics, AdSense loader, AI scripts) must not be altered during presentation edits.

---

## 16. Analytics Standards

- One centralized analytics setup (GA) per property; do not duplicate or fragment.
- Analytics scripts are **protected engineering systems** — content edits never modify them.
- Track meaningful events (search, tool usage, AI guide engagement) without collecting unnecessary personal data.
- Use analytics to find thin/low-value pages for improvement, not to justify dark patterns.

---

## 17. Sitemap / Indexing Standards

- The sitemap contains **only real, public, valid, canonical URLs**.
- **Never** include phantom pages, routes that 404, preview/branch URLs, localhost, internal/QA/NOTES/PLAN/REPORT/MANIFEST/STATUS files, or malformed paths.
- One canonical URL per page; no duplicates in the sitemap.
- robots.txt and sitemap.xml are protected; change them deliberately, never as a side effect.
- Indexing workflow: submit to Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster Tools; use IndexNow for fast updates.
- New pages are added to the sitemap **only after** they are real, reachable, and linked.

---

## 18. Expansion Standards

- Expansion is **coverage of real demand**, not URL inflation.
- Add a city/service page only when it can be real, unique, useful, linked, and canonicalized.
- Expansion math is a planning tool, not a license to generate empty pages (e.g., 750 cities × 8 services = 6,000 **real** pages).
- Every expansion batch passes a QA checklist before sitemap inclusion and IndexNow submission.
- Maintain a coverage dashboard: cities per state, services per city, missing pages, sitemap inclusion, indexed status, monetization status, QA status.

---

## 19. "Never Do" Rules (Platform-Wide, Non-Negotiable)

1. **Never** publish phantom/404/placeholder URLs or list them in the sitemap.
2. **Never** ship thin or duplicate (name-swap) content.
3. **Never** give medical, legal, financial, or insurance advice.
4. **Never** use fear, false scarcity, or manipulation to drive clicks or conversions.
5. **Never** place ads on thin/empty/error pages or in deceptive layouts.
6. **Never** modify protected engineering systems during content/presentation work (AI backend, analytics, AdSense loader, robots, sitemap, Netlify functions, site.js).
7. **Never** hide or remove required disclaimers or attribution.
8. **Never** collect or expose sensitive personal data unnecessarily.
9. **Never** let monetization override user trust.
10. **Never** break internal linking or create orphan pages during edits.
