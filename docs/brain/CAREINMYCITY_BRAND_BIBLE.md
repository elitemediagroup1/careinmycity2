# CAREINMYCITY_BRAND_BIBLE.md

**CareInMyCity Brand Identity**
*The definitive guide to who CareInMyCity is, how it sounds, and how it must feel to families.*

> Inherits all rules from EMG_PLATFORM_BIBLE.md. This document governs CareInMyCity's brand voice and identity.

---

## 1. Mission

CareInMyCity exists to help families confidently navigate senior and family care. We turn one of life's most overwhelming decisions into a clear, manageable process by combining educational resources, AI guidance from Carl, organizational tools like My Care Folder, and trusted public information — all organized locally.

## 2. Vision

A world where no family has to face a care decision alone, confused, or in the dark. Every city, every service, every step — clear, supported, and human.

## 3. Audience

Primary: **adult children (often 45–65)** researching care for an aging parent, frequently under time pressure and emotional stress.

Secondary: spouses/partners acting as caregivers; the seniors themselves; extended family helping from a distance; and professionals (social workers, discharge planners) looking for resources to share.

Mindset of the reader: overwhelmed, time-poor, emotionally invested, skeptical of sales pitches, and looking for trustworthy guidance — not a sale.

## 4. Emotional Tone

CareInMyCity should always feel:

- **Calm** — never frantic or alarmist.
- **Clear** — plain language, no jargon walls.
- **Trustworthy** — sourced, honest, transparent.
- **Warm** — human, compassionate, never cold or clinical.
- **Practical** — actionable next steps, not vague reassurance.
- **Non-alarming** — informs without inducing panic.
- **Non-salesy** — guides without pushing.
- **Family-first** — the family's wellbeing leads every decision.

## 5. Voice

We speak like a knowledgeable, caring friend who happens to understand the care system. We are the steady person in the room who says, "Okay. Here's what this means, and here's what we can do next."

- Second person ("you", "your family") to stay personal.
- Short sentences for clarity in stressful moments.
- Acknowledge emotion, then provide direction.
- Confident but humble — we guide, we don't diagnose or prescribe.

## 6. Vocabulary

**Use:** care options, families, loved one, your parent, guidance, resources, next steps, public resources, when families consider, things to ask, organize, prepare.

**Avoid:** "deals", "best price", "act now", "limited time", "shocking", clinical coldness, fear language, and absolute medical/legal/financial claims.

**Sensitive terms:** prefer "your loved one" / "your parent" over "the elderly" or "the patient". Prefer "memory care" over loaded descriptors. Always person-first.

## 7. Visual Identity

- Keep the current CareInMyCity visual identity and design tokens. Do not introduce a competing design language.
- Calm, accessible palette; generous spacing; readable typography; card-based layouts; consistent button styling.
- Mobile-first and accessible (contrast, alt text, semantic structure).
- Professional iconography only where already used elsewhere on the site.

## 8. Trust Standards

Every page reinforces trust through: visible author/founder attribution, clear sourcing of public/government/nonprofit resources, an honest About page, consistent Organization schema, and visible disclaimers. Trust is the product; everything else supports it.

## 9. Disclaimer Standards

Every relevant page must make clear that CareInMyCity provides **educational information and public resources only**, and **does not provide medical, legal, financial, or insurance advice**. Families should consult qualified professionals for their specific situation. Disclaimers are honest and visible — never buried or removed.

## 10. Brand Promises

1. We will always put your family's wellbeing first.
2. We will tell you the truth, clearly.
3. We will point you to trusted, public resources.
4. We will never use fear to sell.
5. We will help you organize and prepare, at your pace.

## 11. What CareInMyCity Is

- An educational, AI-assisted local care **guidance** platform.
- A directory of care services organized by state, city, and service type.
- A surface for trusted **public** resources (government, nonprofit).
- A toolkit (Carl + My Care Folder) for understanding and organizing care decisions.

## 12. What CareInMyCity Is Not

- Not a medical, legal, financial, or insurance advisor.
- Not a lead-generation pressure machine.
- Not a provider, agency, or broker of care services.
- Not a replacement for professional or personalized advice.
- Not a fear-driven or salesy site.

## 13. Carl's Role in the Brand

Carl is the **AI Care Guide** — the warm, conversational face of CareInMyCity's helpfulness. Carl helps visitors understand care options, organize documents, locate resources, prepare questions, and navigate difficult decisions. Carl is a guide and companion, never an advisor giving professional opinions. (See CAREINMYCITY_AI_CARL_BRAIN.md.)

## 14. My Care Folder's Role

My Care Folder is the brand's promise of **organization and control** made tangible. It helps families store documents, plan, collaborate with other caregivers, and prepare — turning chaos into a manageable, shareable plan.

## 15. How CareInMyCity Should Feel to Families

Like exhaling. Like finally finding someone who gets it, won't judge, won't sell, and will calmly help you take the next step. Relief, clarity, and quiet confidence.

## 16. Examples of Good Copy

- "Choosing care for a parent is one of the hardest decisions a family makes. Let's take it one step at a time."
- "Here's what memory care typically involves, who it may help, and the questions worth asking before you decide."
- "These are public resources in your state that can help — at no cost."
- "Not sure where to start? Carl can help you organize your thoughts and your documents."

## 17. Examples of Bad Copy (Never Write This)

- "Don't wait — your parent's safety is at risk RIGHT NOW!" *(fear-selling)*
- "We've found the BEST DEAL on assisted living near you!" *(salesy, false authority)*
- "Doctors recommend this — guaranteed results." *(false medical claim)*
- "Sign up now before it's too late!" *(false scarcity)*
- "The patient should be placed in a facility immediately." *(cold, advice-giving, non-person-first)*

---

*Calm. Clear. Trustworthy. Warm. Practical. Family-first. If a sentence fails those words, rewrite it.*
