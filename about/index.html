Why CareInMyCity exists

CareInMyCity wasn’t built as a business exercise. It was built from lived experience.

Charlie Brugnolotti has spent two decades in marketing, but the reason this platform exists is far more personal than professional. As a caregiver for a parent with dementia, and a father to a daughter with severe autism, Charlie has lived the confusion, urgency, paperwork, and emotional weight that families face when they’re trying to make care decisions.

He built CareInMyCity to make that search clearer, calmer, and more useful for other families. The goal is simple: help people understand their local options, organize the next steps, and feel a little less alone in the process.

Our Mission

Choosing care is one of the biggest decisions a family will make.

When a parent or loved one needs more support, families are often asked to make complicated, emotional choices quickly — usually with very little guidance. The information is scattered, the terminology is confusing, and the stakes feel enormous.

CareInMyCity exists to simplify that process. We bring together educational resources, AI-guided support, and trusted public information so that families can understand their senior care options clearly and move forward with confidence. Our goal is simple: help you find the next right step, without the noise.

My Care Folder

A calmer way to plan, organize, and share.

Caring for a loved one often means juggling documents, appointments, contacts, and decisions across multiple family members. My Care Folder gives you one private, organized place to keep it all together.

  • Planning tools — checklists and guides for each stage of care.

Editorial Standards

Accuracy and trust you can rely on.

Senior care decisions deserve information you can trust. Here’s how we approach the content across CareInMyCity.

Educational first

Our content is written to inform and explain — never to pressure or sell.

Public resources

We surface and link to publicly available resources families can use directly.

Government sources

Where possible, we draw on official government programs and guidance.

Nonprofit organizations

We reference reputable nonprofits dedicated to seniors and caregivers.

Regular reviews

Content is reviewed and updated to stay accurate and relevant over time.

Not a replacement for professional advice

Our resources support — but never replace — qualified medical, legal, or financial guidance.
